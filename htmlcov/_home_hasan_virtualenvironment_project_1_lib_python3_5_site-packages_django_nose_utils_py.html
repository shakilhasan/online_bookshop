6def process_tests(suite, process): 

7 """Find and process the suite with setup/teardown methods. 

8 

9 Given a nested disaster of [Lazy]Suites, traverse to the first level 

10 that has setup or teardown, and do something to them. 

11 

12 If we were to traverse all the way to the leaves (the Tests) 

13 indiscriminately and return them, when the runner later calls them, they'd 

14 run without reference to the suite that contained them, so they'd miss 

15 their class-, module-, and package-wide setup and teardown routines. 

16 

17 The nested suites form basically a double-linked tree, and suites will call 

18 up to their containing suites to run their setups and teardowns, but it 

19 would be hubris to assume that something you saw fit to setup or teardown 

20 at the module level is less costly to repeat than DB fixtures. Also, those 

21 sorts of setups and teardowns are extremely rare in our code. Thus, we 

22 limit the granularity of bucketing to the first level that has setups or 

23 teardowns. 

24 

25 :arg process: The thing to call once we get to a leaf or a test with setup 

26 or teardown 

27 """ 

28 if (not hasattr(suite, '_tests') or 

29 (hasattr(suite, 'hasFixtures') and suite.hasFixtures())): 

30 # We hit a Test or something with setup, so do the thing. (Note that 

31 # "fixtures" here means setup or teardown routines, not Django 

32 # fixtures.) 

33 process(suite) 

34 else: 

35 for t in suite._tests: 

36 process_tests(t, process)

39def is_subclass_at_all(cls, class_info): 

40 """Return whether ``cls`` is a subclass of ``class_info``. 

41 

42 Even if ``cls`` is not a class, don't crash. Return False instead. 

43 

44 """ 

45 try: 

46 return issubclass(cls, class_info) 

47 except TypeError:
